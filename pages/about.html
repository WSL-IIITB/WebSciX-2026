About WebSciX India

Web Science Trust

The Web Science Trust (WST) is a global initiative dedicated to advancing the understanding of the Web through interdisciplinary research and education. It coordinates the Web Science network (WSTNet), bringing together leading research laboratories worldwide to study socio-technical systems.

History of Web Science

Web Science originated from the Web Science Research Initiative (WSRI) established in 2006 as a collaboration between MIT CSAIL and the University of Southampton. Over time, it evolved into a major interdisciplinary field integrating computing, social sciences, and policy.

Web Science Lab at IIIT Bangalore

The Web Science Lab (WSL) at IIIT Bangalore focuses on understanding how the Internet, WWW, and AI affect human life across domains such as education, governance, and social systems. Originally established as the Open Systems Lab (OSL) in 2002, it is now part of the global WSTNet.

WebSciX: Extending Web Science to India

WebSciX India is a proposed satellite event of the ACM Web Science Conference, extending global Web Science discussions into the Indian context—similar to how TEDx localizes TED.

It builds upon the Web Science for Development (WS4D) series conducted since 2019, focusing on interdisciplinary collaboration and regional challenges.

WebSciX 2026

WebSciX 2026 focuses on the evolving intersection of Web Science and emerging AI paradigms, especially Generative and Agentic AI. It aims to address key issues such as multilingual access, digital inclusion, misinformation, and ethical AI.

Objectives

  • Community Building: Interdisciplinary networks
  • Localized Research: India-specific challenges
  • Innovative Solutions: Scalable interventions
  • Global Integration: Align with global Web Science

Call to Action

WebSciX India invites researchers, technologists, policymakers, and civil society to collaboratively shape the future of Web Science and AI.
