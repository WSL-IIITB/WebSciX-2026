About WebSciX India

Web Science Trust

The Web Science Trust (WST) is a global charitable organization dedicated to advancing the understanding of the Web through interdisciplinary research and education. It coordinates the Web Science network (WSTNet), which comprises leading research laboratories from around the world working on the study of the Web as a complex socio-technical system.

The origins of WST can be traced back to the Web Science Research Initiative (WSRI), established in 2006 through a collaboration between MIT CSAIL and the University of Southampton. WSRI aimed to articulate a research agenda for the broader scientific community, develop educational curricula, and establish thought leadership in this emerging field. Over time, WSRI evolved into the Web Science Trust to expand beyond education and foster a broader research ecosystem.

Today, Web Science is a well-established interdisciplinary domain with annual ACM conferences, peer-reviewed journals, and a global network of researchers and institutions contributing to the understanding of digital ecosystems.

Web Science and its Interdisciplinary Foundations

Web Science integrates perspectives from computer science, sociology, economics, policy studies, and other disciplines to understand the evolving relationship between technology and society. The field recognizes that the Web is not merely a technical artifact, but a socio-technical system shaped by human behavior, governance, and economic structures.

The establishment of Web Science has opened new research directions across multiple domains, enabling technologists to contribute to social research while allowing social scientists to engage deeply with technological systems.

Web Science Lab (WSL) at IIIT Bangalore

The Web Science Lab (WSL) at IIIT Bangalore is a key contributor to the global Web Science ecosystem and is part of the Web Science Trust Network (WSTNet). The lab focuses on understanding how the Internet, the World Wide Web, and Artificial Intelligence are transforming various aspects of human life, including business, governance, education, and social interactions.

Originally established as the Open Systems Lab (OSL) in 2002, WSL has evolved into a center for interdisciplinary research exploring socio-technical systems. The lab has also contributed to initiatives such as digital empowerment through partnerships with organizations like Gooru Learning Inc., and has been involved in the co-creation of the Centre of Excellence in Big Data Engineering.

The foundational ideas of the lab are also influenced by early work such as “The Power Law of Information: Life in a Connected World,” which anticipated many developments in modern digital ecosystems even before the rise of social media and mobile platforms.

WebSciX India: A Regional Extension of Web Science

WebSciX India is a proposed satellite event of the ACM Web Science Conference, designed to extend the global Web Science movement into the Indian context. Much like how TEDx localizes TED conferences, WebSciX serves as a platform to contextualize global research themes within regional challenges and opportunities.

WebSciX builds upon the Web Science for Development (WS4D) series of workshops conducted in India since 2019. These workshops have played a foundational role in initiating interdisciplinary discussions around the impact of the Web and digital technologies in developing regions.

India’s Digital Landscape

With over 800 million Internet users as of 2025, India represents one of the largest and most diverse digital ecosystems in the world. Its 1.4 billion population, multilingual environment, and rapid technological adoption present both significant opportunities and complex challenges.

India has made substantial progress in areas such as digital identity systems, digital payments, technology-enabled education, and large-scale public digital infrastructure. At the same time, persistent challenges such as digital divides, misinformation, privacy concerns, and uneven access to technology highlight the need for a localized Web Science approach.

In this context, WebSciX India aims to:

  • Address region-specific challenges such as multilingual access, rural connectivity, and digital literacy
  • Examine the impact of AI and the Web on sectors including commerce, education, and governance
  • Develop inclusive and scalable solutions for equitable digital access

WebSciX 2026

WebSciX 2026 marks the next step in this evolving initiative, focusing on the intersection of Web Science and emerging paradigms such as Generative and Agentic AI. The conference seeks to explore how these technologies are reshaping digital ecosystems and influencing societal outcomes.

The event aims to bring together researchers, technologists, policymakers, and civil society stakeholders to collaboratively address pressing challenges and shape the future of digital systems in India and beyond.

Objectives

  • Community Building: Establish a regional interdisciplinary network of researchers and practitioners
  • Localized Research: Promote research addressing India-specific socio-technical challenges
  • Innovative Solutions: Develop scalable technological and policy interventions
  • Global-Local Integration: Align India’s priorities with global Web Science research

Call to Action

WebSciX India invites researchers, technologists, policymakers, and civil society organizations to join this initiative. By fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and contextual research, WebSciX aims to ensure that the Internet and AI contribute meaningfully to inclusive and equitable societal development.
