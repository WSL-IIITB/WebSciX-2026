About WebSciX India

Web Science Trust

The Web Science Trust (WST) is a global initiative dedicated to advancing the understanding of the Web through interdisciplinary research and education. It coordinates the Web Science network (WSTNet), bringing together leading research laboratories worldwide to study the evolving socio-technical systems of the Internet.

History of Web Science

The origins of Web Science trace back to the Web Science Research Initiative (WSRI), established in 2006 as a collaboration between MIT CSAIL and the University of Southampton. The initiative aimed to formalize the scientific study of the World Wide Web and its societal implications.

Over time, Web Science has evolved into a major interdisciplinary field, integrating computer science, sociology, economics, and policy studies. To expand its scope, WSRI transitioned into the Web Science Trust, which now supports global research, conferences, and educational programs.

Web Science Lab at IIIT Bangalore

The Web Science Lab (WSL) at IIIT Bangalore focuses on understanding how the Internet, World Wide Web, and Artificial Intelligence influence various aspects of human life. These technologies have transformed business, governance, education, and social systems, yet their deeper human implications remain underexplored.

WSL is part of the global Web Science Trust Network (WSTNet) and contributes to advancing research on socio-technical systems. Originally established as the Open Systems Lab (OSL) in 2002, it has evolved into a leading center for interdisciplinary research in Web Science.

WebSciX: Extending Web Science to India

WebSciX India is a proposed satellite event of the ACM Web Science Conference, designed to extend the global Web Science movement into the Indian context. Much like how TEDx extends TED, WebSciX brings localized, region-specific discussions to a global research framework.

Building on the Web Science for Development (WS4D) workshop series conducted since 2019, WebSciX India aims to foster collaboration across disciplines and address challenges unique to India’s digital ecosystem.

Progress and Previous Editions

India’s digital landscape, with over 800 million Internet users, presents both immense opportunities and complex challenges. Prior WS4D workshops and WebSciX initiatives have explored themes such as digital inclusion, misinformation, and socio-technical governance.

  • WS4D 2019 – Foundational discussions on Web Science in India
  • WS4D 2020–2024 – Expanding interdisciplinary engagement
  • WebSciX 2025 – Consolidating research directions and collaborations

WebSciX 2026

WebSciX 2026 continues this trajectory by focusing on the intersection of Web Science and emerging AI paradigms, particularly Generative and Agentic AI. The conference aims to address key challenges such as multilingual access, digital inclusion, misinformation, and ethical AI deployment in India.

By bringing together researchers, technologists, policymakers, and civil society, WebSciX 2026 seeks to build a strong regional ecosystem while contributing to the global Web Science community.

Objectives

  • Community Building: Establish interdisciplinary networks across academia, industry, and policy.
  • Localized Research: Address India-specific challenges such as DPI, inclusion, and privacy.
  • Innovative Solutions: Develop scalable socio-technical interventions.
  • Global Integration: Align India’s research with global Web Science initiatives.

Call to Action

WebSciX India invites researchers, technologists, policymakers, and civil society to join this initiative. Through interdisciplinary collaboration and contextual research, we aim to ensure that the Internet and AI empower and uplift communities across India and beyond.
