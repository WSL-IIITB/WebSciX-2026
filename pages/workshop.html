About the Workshops

WebSciX 2026 will host a dedicated workshop day as part of its three-day programme. Workshops are half-day or full-day focused sessions designed to bring together researchers and practitioners around emerging or specialized topics that benefit from deeper discussion than a regular conference session allows.

Confirmed Workshop

Consciousness Studies in the New Age of AI

The topic of consciousness, despite being hard to even define, has today attracted much research attention from a diversity of domains spanning philosophy, science, technology, psychology, and spirituality. Consensus is gradually emerging in the scientific community on the proposal that consciousness, whichever way we describe our subjective experience of it, may not be a product or property of the material world that makes up our life form and our brains.

Notions of consciousness have been rooted in Indic thought over millennia, and many ideas drawn from ancient writings seem to resonate well with modern lines of inquiry into topics that fall squarely in emerging AI. It is not surprising to see scientists working in AI and robotics grappling with terms such as 'self', 'awareness', 'being', consciousness, ethics, and responsible action — terms that seem native to the vast Indic literature of our ancient land. With the prospect of AGI emerging from modern AI technologies sooner than later, the question on whether AI-driven agents and robots can turn conscious is raging at the present time.

The objective of the workshop is to constructively explore ideas rooted in Indic thought and literature which may assist and enrich our understanding of the challenges facing research in this area.

This workshop is supported by The Chittosha Collective, an informal group of researchers at IIIT Bangalore.

Confirmed Workshop

Digital Personal Data Protection & Digital Public Infrastructure

Digital Public Infrastructures (DPI) are increasingly shaping the delivery of public services - identity systems, payments, health platforms, and data exchanges across the world. As DPI ecosystems expand, consent management has emerged as a concern for enabling trustworthy, transparent, and user-centric data sharing. At the same time, the rise of generative and agentic AI systems introduces new challenges around informed consent, automated decision-making, and cross-platform accountability.This half-day workshop brings together researchers, developers, policymakers, civil society practitioners, and DPI implementers to collaborate and brainstorm on different challenges while designing DPIs.The workshop aims to foster interdisciplinary dialogue while identifying future research directions for trustworthy DPIs in the age of generative and agentic AI. In this spirit we would also like to invite people working in the domain to send a short paper on their work. This workshop explores the emerging challenges and opportunities of consent management in Digital Public Infrastructures (DPIs) in the era of generative and agentic AI. Bringing together researchers, developers, policymakers, and practitioners, it will examine human-centered consent design, interoperable trust architectures, privacy-preserving technologies, explainability, delegated consent, and cross-sector data sharing. The half-day interactive program will feature a keynote, lightning talks, breakout discussions, and a live demonstration of the **Anumati** platform, fostering dialogue on trustworthy AI-enabled governance. The workshop aims to build a cross-disciplinary community, identify future research directions, develop recommendations for standards and best practices, and encourage collaborative research on secure, transparent, and trustworthy consent ecosystems. We invite position papers, case studies, demonstrations, and discussion proposals from theoretical, technical, empirical, and system design perspectives.
